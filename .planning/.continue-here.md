## Probe 4 — Active Hypothesis: Hypothesis B

Flame's hook scanner walks the entire `/opt/Autodesk/shared/python/` tree at startup, including depth 3+ paths like `forge_core/solver/`, `forge_core/math/`, `forge_core/image/`, `forge_core/colour/`. It treats every `.py` and `__init__.py` it finds as a candidate hook, attempts to import each, and logs noise when imports fail (e.g. solver.py's `from .fitting import ...` relative import fails when imported as a bare top-level script).

**These hook-scanner warnings have been there forever.** They are NOT the cause of the SIGSEGV. They are pre-existing noise that just happens to share the console with the actual crash output.

**The actual SIGSEGV is something Phase-04.3-specific** that surfaces when forge-bridge's `exec` endpoint calls `action.import_fbx()` with the post-Phase-04.3-generated FBX. It does NOT happen pre-Phase-04.3 (per `cbfd185` HUMAN-UAT note: "round-trip is functional as of commit cbfd185").
