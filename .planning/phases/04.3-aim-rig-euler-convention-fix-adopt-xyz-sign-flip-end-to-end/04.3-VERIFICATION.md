## H. Deviation: Amendment §A's Symbolic Derivation Was Wrong

The plan amendment claimed the empirically-correct convention is
`R = Rx(-rx) · Ry(-ry) · Rz(-rz)` (X·Y·Z matrix-product order, all
three signs negated). I verified this numerically against Camera1
during Task 3 and found:

| Convention applied to Camera1 look-at output | rx | ry | rz | max |Δ| vs Flame truth |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Amendment's X·Y·Z form | 1.837° | 1.017° | 1.285° | 0.046° |
| **Original plan's Z·Y·X form** | **1.814°** | **1.058°** | **1.252°** | **0.006°** |

The amendment author was correct that the L167 sign flip is needed,
but interpreted scipy's extrinsic `xyz` mode incorrectly: extrinsic
`xyz` is equivalent to applying `Rz·Ry·Rx` (Z·Y·X product), not
`Rx·Ry·Rz`. The original plan's `<symbolic_derivation_for_xyz_decomposer>`
block was actually correct.

**Auto-applied Rule 1 fix:** Used the original plan's Z·Y·X derivation
in the final Task 3 atomic commit. The Task 2 commit (fdadf65) shipped
the X·Y·Z bodies; Task 3 (5d5febe) corrects them. Functionally identical
end state — only the intermediate Task 2 commit had buggy code, and
no callers were wired to it at that point so behavior was unaffected.

The Camera1 known-answer test in Task 2 was `xfail`'d, which is what
made the bug invisible until the look-at coupling landed. In Task 3 the
xfail was removed and the test passed (after also correcting the body).
